Creating a Function Generator

28 April 2022 | Circuits

A function generator is usually a piece of electronic test equipment or software used to generate different types of electrical waveforms over a wide range of frequencies. Some of the most common waveforms produced by the function generator are the sine wave, square wave, triangular wave and sawtooth shapes. These waveforms can be either repetitive or single-shot (which requires an internal or external trigger source).Integrated circuits used to generate waveforms may also be described as function generator ICs.

Designing an Intelligent Traffic Signal

15 March 2022 | Digital Logic

The target of the project is to build an intelligent traffic signal for a four-way intersection. Such a system will help reduce traffic congestion in busy areas and enhance public transport efficiency. It will achieve such a feat by scaling the waiting times at each signal with their respective traffic load. The intuition behind our intelligent traffic signal design is based on using a 2-bit counter that loops around 0 to 3 (road 0, road 1, road 2, road 3 and reset through a 4 state catcher) to cycle around the four roads, one by one. The 2-bit counter is operated by a 0.2 Hz clock, meaning it will increment after every 5 seconds. In other words, during the cycling process, it will stay on each road for 5 seconds before moving to the next. The output of the counter will be fed into a 2-to-4 decoder to extract the four minterms.

Space Settlement Design

2018 Dashboard

Scientists believe that once the first settlement is built in space, and a mining, refining, manufacturing, and transportation infrastructure is established, economics and ingenuity will enable large settlements to be built in fewer than 15 years. This is akin to a “Southern California freeway phenomenon”, wherein a new freeway is built into a remote area with very little population, and in just a few years the freeway is clogged with traffic because the access it provides enables communities and businesses to be established on its flanks. Having infrastructure in place makes it easier to get more infrastructure in place.
